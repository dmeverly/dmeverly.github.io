Design Principles

  • Identify the Problem
    The most important step in designing solutions is to gain a complete understanding of the problem in context.
  • User-Centered Design
    Users are an important part of the system, and they are under no obligation to use the system as-intended.
  • Flexibility for Extension
    Requirements change over time. Build component boundaries that allow systems to evolve without extensive refactoring.
  • Maintenance
    Maintenance costs more than development. A thoughtfully-designed solution saves money over time.
  • Security First
    Real-world systems are under constant threat. Design with security as a fundamental principle.
