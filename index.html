About

I design and evaluate intelligent systems for high-stakes environments where ambiguity, partial availability, and downstream risk are the norm. My approach assumes pressure, misuse, and failure from the outset — prioritizing controlled behavior, interpretability, and operational resilience over novelty.

This perspective was forged in critical care, where system failures are not theoretical and time pressure is constant. My work now spans AI systems, orchestration frameworks, and security-adjacent domains where correctness, trust boundaries, and failure containment matter.

Design Principles

  • Design for non-ideal conditions
    Systems will be stressed, partially unavailable, or used incorrectly.
  • Availability matters
    Degraded modes beat outages; resilience reduces downstream risk.
  • Design for misuse and adversarial inputs
    Correctness includes hostile and unexpected behavior.
  • Prefer robust abstractions over cleverness
    Build for clarity, scale, and long-term evolution.
  • Expect requirements to change
    Build boundaries that allow systems to evolve without rewrites.
  • Prefer conservative correctness over confident failure
    Silent refusal can be safer than plausible wrongness.
