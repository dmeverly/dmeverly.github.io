About

I'm a Software Engineer and practicing Acute Care Nurse Practitioner with over a decade of experience in Critical Care and Emergency Medicine.


My professional interests are centered around applied artificial intelligence, healthcare innovation, and security.

I am drawn to risk mitigation strategies, especially in critical systems. I study offensive security techniques to develop proactive and resilient defense strategies.

Extensive real-world clinical experience has given me a deep understanding of risk, trust, and safety. Now, I leverage this experience to design software systems that are intentionally built to streamline workflows and defend against cybersecurity threats.

Much of my work focuses on understanding risk and designing systems that mitigate potential harm.

Design Principles

  • Design for the Real World
    Systems will be stressed, used incorrectly, and under attack.
  • Availability Matters
    Degraded modes beat outages; resilience reduces downstream risk.
  • Correctness over Speed
    Build for clarity, scale, and long-term evolution.
  • Requirements Change
    Build boundaries that allow systems to evolve without rewrites.
  • Zero Trust
    Refusal by default. Compliance by exception.
